John Hart

Group Director, HR

Interviewed on 30/8/2001 at Westwood
Present: Paul Rutter (Powergen); John Wilson and Lorenzo Wood (Oyster)

Personal

  • Needs a database on everyone in the company (mostly managers).
  • Need good, reliable mobile communications.

Relations with group directors

  • Having a “lean and mean” group requires stronger relationships. Relationships are critical.

Group Function

  • “To look exclusively at the group rather than at the group + the function is wrong.”
  • “We haven’t yet recognised the inefficiencies of how we use the technology”
  • The process of working out how to work together better should be done collaboratively.
  • We should be behaviourally led, not technologically led. Staff commitment is not automatic – we must not do what Bob Ayling did [at British Airways].

“Virtual HR”

  • “What I need to deliver with a team of six I cannot deliver without the cooperation of the businesses.”
  • Principles of vHR: removal of duplication; accountability at the point of capability; self-service information maintenance.
  • “[We have a culture of] information hiding because people do not want to be second-guessed. Information is a corporate asset.”
  • Using/going to use [which?] Rebus (see http://www.rebushr.com)
  • “How do you build infrastructure that doesn’t impede educational change?”
  • Group HR develops products/services (as opposed to functional support [meaning what?]); once a product or service has become a commodity, buy it in Þ“I can envisage a situation where no HR services are provided by the company [but it will need technology to provide access to the bought-in services]”
  • [Group HR therefore] provides “heavy hitting” stuff such as change management.

M&A

  • Need to make sure that the resources exist to handle a proposed M&A (hence the need for a database). Need to know we can deliver sufficient resources with the right skills at the right time [at “the drop of a hat” – see Caroline Sheers interview].

Succession Planning

  • Need information about the needs of succession planning (local vs. HR) [meaning?]
  • Need the ability to challenge the businesses on the experience of their [human(!)] stock. For example: “Is your succession plan adequate?” “Can you facilitate the development of X in Y way?”

Adoption & Training

  • Ego vs. training: not “I can’t use this…” but “I don’t use this, because…”. How much do people know about the things that they can do already?

Other Issues

Internal Communications

  • “Are we satisfied that the communication processes we are using are capturing the imagination of everyone in the company?”
  • The concepts are not obvious (what about communicating them?)
  • Bricks’n’Clicks CD-ROM was “superficial and not exciting”.
  • “How are we getting everyone to understand the same concept?”

Email

  • Powergen needs e-mail etiquette

Coaching

  • We use this word a lot; what do we mean by it? What do the businesses think?
  • JH to pick up defining buzzwords (eg, “merger of equals” – referring to Powergen/LG&E – resulted in broken promises).
