Audrey Barrow

PA to Caroline Sheers

Interviewed on 13/9/2001 at New Broad Street
Present: Lorenzo Wood (Oyster)

How do you spend your working time?

Mostly normal working hours — sometimes longer if Caroline needs it

Diary management

  • Handles all diary changes.
  • Very dynamic. Just switching over to an electronic diary (as of 17/9) [how is she doing with it now?]
  • 80% of bookings requested by Caroline; 20% in response to requests from other people.
  • Requests mostly dealt with by phone – “I prefer to speak to people – [if you send e-mail, for example,] you don’t know if they’re there.”
  • 60% of meetings internal, 40% with external people (but depends on current projects)

Telephoning

  • Making calls.
  • Answering calls and putting them through to Caroline if appropriate.
  • Caroline leaves voicemail messages for Audrey. The other way, Audrey can usually track Caroline down.

E-mails

  • Access to Caroline’s inbox.
  • Reads e-mails; prints off key ones and walks hands them to Caroline (“have you seen this?”) or just draws attention to them verbally
  • Deals with meeting requests (can send e-mail pp Caroline)
  • When Caroline is away, reads e-mails over the phone and/or prints them and faxes them (“Caroline tends to use her [laptop] when away, but it never works” – Audrey says she has never received an e-mail from Caroline when remote).

What could be better about your job?

  • Quite happy.
  • Could work more for the team [M&A team?], but don’t have time, and there isn’t enough work to have two people for Caroline to give Audrey the space.
  • “Caroline likes an ‘old fashioned’ secretary, but would like me to me more ‘techy’”

What do you think are Caroline’s issues?

  • She tends to lose everything, so a one-piece machine (ie, without any cables, adaptors, etc.) would be a boon.
  • Sitting close together [with other group directors] would/will be good.
  • “I think we’re getting an iPaq”
  • “Caroline is more techy than I; probably knows more”

Other points

Kim’s PA group

  • Not much use really. Has no authority. Is never realistically going to get everyone together. “Just an excuse for a load of women to sit around with coffee and biscuits.”
