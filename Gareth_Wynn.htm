Gareth Wynn

Group Director, Corporate Communications

Interviewed on 6/9/2001 at New Broad Street
Present: Paul Rutter (Powergen); John Wilson and Lorenzo Wood (Oyster)

Personal

  • Needs to be able to “get into Powergen systems (e-mail, shared drives) from anywhere, without necessarily carrying a laptop”;
  • Needs strong communication with team (eg, through increasing use of videoconferencing);
  • Needs good communication with press, but their technology adoption is generally very low (Þ faxing from the desktop may be useful).

Relations with group directors

  • “[they need to] be more receptive to change… [we need to] agree values and then stick to them – act like we mean them – …if everyone goes back into their silos and doesn’t change behaviour, it won’t work.”
  • Response to issues: phone call to Nick Baldwin, Peter Hickson and David Jackson, plus heads of UK and US businesses if necessary.
  • Informal and goal-oriented working methods (eg, if you need to talk to someone, go and see her/him) – better way of working, but doesn’t help standing among colleagues.
  • Training in handling broadcast media infrequent, involving professional newscaster Þ could use more modular, at-desk “refresher” training)

Group Function

  • Largely consists of two functions - Reactive (responding to issues) and Proactive (planning and PR training):

Reactive

  • Coordinating function – managing the escalation of issues through the businesses Þ delegate to business communications functions and educate them about what is “share price sensitive” (to make GW’s increasing proportion of travel time – especially on aircraft when he is out of touch – less of a problem).
  • “I add value when the shit’s hitting the fan”
  • Intelligence comes from: net alerts (aware, although not clear how much actually used) and broadcast monitoring (eg, Reuters) Þ have asked Infonic to suggest a couple of “nuggets” of deeper knowledge by way of illustration of the possibilities of smarter Internet monitoring.
  • On the output side: uses a fulfilment house (United News Media) to do bulk faxing Þ could save money by faxing press releases directly.
  • All press releases go out on the Web and on the intranet.

Proactive

  • Training in media relations (see group directors section above);
  • No consolidated communications plan, but GW “trusted to distil what the businesses need”;
  • Plan for individual campaigns and act as a coordinator for release (cf. Barclays disaster, where three perfectly reasonable plans were executed at the same time, creating a very bad effect).

Other Issues

  • Random Idea - Decentralisation in reverse; stop the businesses from replicating functions that should be done within the group.
  • Measurement should be communicated to the organisation - Amount of press releases; coverage of Powergen’s community initiatives; share of coverage.
  • E.ON - Currently GW speaks three of four times per week but meets about once per month; “Keen but backward” (eg, enthusiastic response to, and consequent wish to replicate, Clicks@home initiative; “enthusiastic pockets” of activity)
  • Internal Communications - Sharing best practice; mechanisms for group (eg, delivering the same (consistent) message in different ways); helping an understanding of the “big picture”.
